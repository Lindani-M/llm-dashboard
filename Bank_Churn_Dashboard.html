Executive Summary

Churn position: The overall churn rate stands at 20.4% (2,037 of 10,000 customers). Germany shows a disproportionately high churn rate of 32.4%, nearly double that of France (16.2%) and Spain (16.7%). Female customers churn at 25.1% vs. 16.5% for males. The most alarming signal: customers with 3+ products churn at 82.7–100%, suggesting a cross-sell strategy that may be backfiring. Inactive members churn at nearly twice the rate of active members (26.9% vs. 14.3%), and the 40–59 age band is the highest-risk demographic. Churned customers carry 25% higher balances on average ($91K vs $73K), representing significant revenue at risk.

Strategic Recommendations

Immediate priorities: (1) Investigate and address the Germany churn crisis — 814 departures at 32.4% demand a regional intervention. (2) Cap cross-sell at 2 products — the data unequivocally shows that 3+ products accelerate churn. (3) Launch targeted re-engagement campaigns for inactive members, prioritising the 40–59 age band where churn peaks at 30–56%. (4) Address the gender gap in retention: female churn at 25.1% vs. 16.5% male warrants service experience analysis. (5) Protect high-balance customers — churned customers carry $91K average balances, representing disproportionate revenue loss per departure.
